Tax Implications on Outward Remittances – India (FY 2025)

1. What is TCS on Outward Remittance?

  • TCS (Tax Collected at Source) is collected by banks or authorized dealers when funds are sent abroad under LRS.
  • It helps the government monitor foreign outflows and tax compliance.
  • TCS is not an additional tax; it can be claimed against your final tax liability or as a refund.

2. TCS Thresholds & Rates for FY 2025

Threshold: ₹10 lakh/year (combined for all remittances per PAN)

Purpose TCS Rate Threshold
Education (loan-financed) NIL Any amount

3. Key Exemptions

  • Loan-financed education: No TCS.
  • Medical or education (non-loan) under ₹10L: No TCS.
  • Foreign spending via international credit cards while overseas: No TCS (as of now).
  • Investments in foreign ETFs/mutual funds: No TCS (outside LRS).
  • Business/corporate outward remittances: Not covered under LRS.

4. Claiming & Adjusting TCS

  • TCS is visible in Form 26AS, AIS, and Form 27D.
  • It is adjustable against your income tax while filing ITR (typically ITR-2 or ITR-3).
  • No interest is paid on held TCS, so it may impact liquidity.

5. Other Key LRS Regulations

  • PAN is mandatory for all LRS transactions.
  • Total limit under LRS: USD 250,000 per person/year.
  • Multiple remittances allowed, but cumulative limit applies.
  • Banks may request ITR or source proof for new customers in capital account transactions.
