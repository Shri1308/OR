Outward Remittances in India: A Comprehensive Overview (FY'25)

Introduction

Outward remittances from India have emerged as a significant facet of the country's external financial activity. Regulated under the Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), this market facilitates the transfer of funds by Indian residents to foreign destinations for varied purposes—ranging from education and travel to investments and gifts.

Countries Receiving Outward Remittances from India

  • Nepal and Bangladesh – Dominant due to proximity and familial connections.
  • USA, UK, and Canada – Common for education and investment, though declined in FY'25.
  • Other countries – For medical, tourism, investments, etc.

Country-wise Percentage Breakdown

  • Travel and Tourism: 57% in FY'25 ($16.96B)
  • Education: 12% in April 2025; $2.92B in FY'25
  • Investment in Foreign Assets: 12.51% growth to $1.699B
  • Maintenance of Relatives: 16%
  • Gifts: 7%

Why Remittance Is Needed During Travel

  • Large-value travel arrangements (e.g. tour packages)
  • Complies with RBI and tax regulations
  • Cards/cash have limitations and different TCS rules

Market Size and Trends

  • FY'25: $29.56B (down from $31.73B)
  • Growth: CAGR ~40% since FY'14
  • Major Categories: Travel ($16.96B), Education ($2.92B), Investments ($1.7B)

Tax Collected at Source (TCS)

  • Threshold: ₹10 lakh per PAN
  • Travel Packages: 20% TCS above ₹10L
  • Education/Medical: 5% TCS (if not via loan)
  • Loan-based Education: 0%

Banks in Outward Remittance

Authorized Dealers process most transactions via SWIFT. Charges include processing, SWIFT, correspondent bank fees, and GST. Axis Bank handled 64% digitally in FY'25.

Top Banks

  • Axis Bank – High digital usage
  • ICICI, HDFC, SBI – Popular with good forex services
  • IDFC First, Kotak, YES Bank – Emerging in remittance convenience
  • Fintechs: Karbon (business), moneyHOP (students/NRIs)

Most Travel-Related Destinations

No official breakdown, but Southeast Asia, Middle East, and Western countries are key travel regions targeted by financial products.

Key Takeaways

  • Total outward remittances: $29.56B (FY'25)
  • Travel is the largest use case (57%)
  • No official country-wise breakdown; Nepal/Bangladesh lead overall
  • TCS applies beyond ₹10L (up to 20%)
  • Digital banking and fintechs are rising rapidly
